## 1. PROJECT OVERVIEW

### Concept
**Hero's Journey** is a gamified health app designed to improve treatment adherence in children undergoing long-term chemotherapy by transforming their medical journey into an empowering hero's quest.

### Target User
- **Primary:** Children 6-14 years old with long-term cancer treatment (6+ months)
- **Secondary:** Parents/guardians, oncologists, medical staff
- **Focus User:** Yasmine, 9 years old, acute leukemia, 36 chemotherapy sessions planned

---

## 2. PROBLEM STATEMENT

**Challenge:**
- 40% of pediatric cancer patients experience significant motivation drop after 2-3 months of treatment
- Children struggle with anxiety, nausea, fatigue, and social isolation
- Traditional medical apps lack emotional connection and engagement
- Patients feel powerless in a process where they have no control

**Opportunity:**
Transform the patient role from "sick child" to "courageous hero" through:
- Emotional connection with a digital companion
- Visual progress and achievement recognition
- Real family support and encouragement
- Age-appropriate gamification mechanics

## 7. ACCESSIBILITY FEATURES

### Physical Accessibility
- Large touch targets (minimum 44x44 pixels)
- Simplified navigation (3 clicks max to any feature)
- One-handed gameplay possible
- Clear visual feedback for all interactions

### Cognitive Accessibility
- Simple language, 8th-grade reading level
- Clear icons with text labels
- No hidden features or confusing menus
- Options to hide some features (less overwhelm)

### Sensory Accessibility
- **Vision:** Dyslexia-friendly font option, low-vision mode, high contrast
- **Hearing:** All audio optional, captions for voiceover
- **Color:** No red-green combinations, accessible color contrast

### Neurodivergence Support
- **ADHD:** Visible progress, immediate feedback, exciting animations
- **Autism:** Predictable structure, minimal surprises, clear rules
- **Anxiety:** No time pressure, no punishment, safe pace
- **Depression:** Low-effort missions, acknowledgment of small wins

## 14. DESIGN DECISIONS & RATIONALE

### Why a Dragon?
- Powerful but friendly archetype
- Culturally significant (Asian representation)
- Clearly non-human (imaginative escape from reality)
- Evolves visually (perfect for progress metaphor)
- Can wear accessories (character customization)

### Why Quest Map?
- Visual progress is powerful for children
- Islands = discrete, manageable sessions (not overwhelming)
- Milestones at 5, 15, 36 (psychological progression)
- Fantasy setting reframes medical journey

### Why No Punishments?
- Children in treatment already experience enough pain
- Fear/guilt = worse adherence outcomes
- Failure to complete = often medical necessity (child too sick)
- Punishment triggers depression and withdrawal

### Why Family Wall?
- Medical studies show family support = 3x better outcomes
- Isolation is major contributor to depression
- Real love is more motivating than gamification
- Builds accountability in healthy way

### Why 1 Notification/Day Max?
- Too many notifications = fatigue and ignorance
- Children deal with enough medical alerts
- Respects peaceful times
- Quality over quantity
